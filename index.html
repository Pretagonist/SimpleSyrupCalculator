Why the volumes don't add up

Sugar dissolves into the water, tucking between water molecules, so 100 g sugar + 100 mL water ≠ 200 mL — it weighs 200 g but measures only ~163 mL. This tool computes in mass (the ratio is by weight), then converts total mass to volume using the measured density of a sucrose solution at 20 °C, interpolated from standard NBS/ICUMSA data.
