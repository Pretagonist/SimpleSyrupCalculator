The science — why volumes don't add up

Dissolving sugar in water doesn't increase the volume by the dry volume of the sugar — the sugar molecules tuck into spaces between water molecules. So 100 g sugar + 100 mL water ≠ 200 mL. It weighs 200 g, but measures only about 163 mL.
